About The Project

Project Overview and Goals

The goal of this project is to assess four different machine learning models to determine which one is the most accurate. Our team has chosen to carry out this assesment on Census Income Data courtesy of UCI's Machine Learning Repository. This dataset contains 15 input factors that can be used to indicate whether ones' income level is above or below $50,000. By training each model on this dataset, our team is able to determine which model can most accurately predict the income level, and which is ready to perform on new data.

To accomplish this project, and to be in-line with certain requirements, our team brought together a number of different coding languages, strategies, and libraries. We facilitated Scikit-Learn as well as Python in a Jupyter Notebook to perform a small amount of cleanup on the dataset and to run our Machine Learning Models. In addition to that, our team built out this webpage for our project using HTML/CSS/Bootstrap and hosted it using a Flask Server on Heroku.

After some deliberation and research, our team selected the following four machine learning models to focus on for this project:

  • Logistic Regression
  • K Nearest Neighbors
  • Random Forest
  • Neural Network

A Deeper Dive into the Data

In an effort to dive deeper into our data, after looking at each of these models, our team turned our focus to the input factors of our dataset. We sought to analyze the strengths of the inputs to determine which, if any, were having a more significant impact on the outcomes of our models. We understand that these findings would not affect which model is the best performer, but chose this additional analysis on our dataset as we felt it would lead to a deeper understanding of our data, our models, and our project as a whole.

Presenting the Project

To display our project and our findings, the team has elected to design a webpage and host it using Heroku's services. We desire for our work to be easily accessible and therefore made this decision to host on a public space that is accessible to anyone who has the link to the webpage. The vision for this webpage is a one-stop-shop where anyone can come along, read about the project, read the analysis of each model, try out our best performing model with our interactive demo, and even connect with the team behind it all.
